Photogrammetry

Capture in photogrammetry of collection items in inventory or directly on site

We produce and optimize faithful models of your works. Work carried out in partnership with the 3D Digitization department of the University of Lille.

More than the performance of the sensor, it is the lighting and the choice of lens that will determine the quality of the final result.

{% picture grid img/photogrammetry/macro.webp --alt Photographing a small object for photogrammetry processing %}

For every size

This technique is suited for models from a few centimeters (macro photography) to several tens of meters (drone photography).

Photogrammetry produces high-quality textures with excellent results on matte surfaces.

Polarized-light protocols also make it possible to digitize shiny objects.

{% picture grid img/photogrammetry/artec_leo.webp --alt Use of an Artec Leo, a handheld structured-light scanner, to digitize a lapidary collection %}

Structured-light scanner

Faster capture that is less dependent on lighting, but some details may be lost during acquisition

While acquisition is much faster and the geometry of high quality (±0.3mm), the texture rendering is generally inferior to that of photogrammetry.
